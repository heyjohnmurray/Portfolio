Rethinking Everything

If it's not broken then why fix it? Besides the fact that the old design looked outdated, the codebase was hacked together from a previous coder who worked on the site. As a result, the site needed to be redone from the ground up on the code side. So it seemed like a great time to rethink the design as well.

Beating this existing design was a frustrating process. Previous attempts to do so continued to fail, which made it hard for us to update the code. We couldn't just turn our back on a winning experience regardless of the code. So we figured the best way to beat the control was to study it and find a way to beat it at its own game.

In the end, we reduced the dual entry to a single entry. This steamlined the process and made it clearer to the user what we expected from them. We used the other side, where the other entry used to be, to provide valuable content to encourage the user to enter their ZIP code.

Lead Form Page

Once the user got to the lead form page, we kept the form the same but surrounded it with more value propositions. We found that adding a red urgency banner increased call in response.
